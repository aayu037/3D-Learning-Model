Appearance:

Lions are muscular, deep-chested cats with short, rounded heads, reduced necks, and round ears. Males are easily recognizable by their thick manes, which can range in color from light blonde to dark brown.

Size:

Lions are the second largest big cats after tigers. Males typically weigh between 150 to 250 kg (330 to 550 lbs), and females weigh between 120 to 182 kg (265 to 400 lbs).

Habitat:

Lions are primarily found in Africa, with a small population in the Gir Forest of India. Their habitats include savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands.

Behavior:

Lions are the most social of all big cats and live in groups called prides, typically consisting of related females and their offspring, along with a few adult males.

Diet:

Lions are carnivores, primarily hunting large herbivores like antelopes, zebras, and wildebeests. They can consume up to 40 kg (88 lbs) of meat in one sitting.

Reproduction:

Lions mate throughout the year, with a gestation period of around 110 days, and a lioness typically gives birth to 1 to 4 cubs.

Conservation Status:

Lions are considered vulnerable, with fewer than 20,000 individuals left in the wild due to habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and poaching.

Interesting Facts:

Lions can reach speeds of up to 50 miles per hour (80 km/h) in short bursts, and a lion's roar can be heard up to 5 miles (8 km) away.
