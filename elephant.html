Welcome to the Elephant 3D Model!
Appearance:

Elephants are large mammals known for their long trunks, large ears, and tusks. Their skin is thick and gray, and they have a unique wrinkled texture.

Size:

Elephants are the largest land animals. Adult males can weigh between 4,500 to 6,800 kg (10,000 to 15,000 lbs) and measure up to 4 meters (13 feet) tall at the shoulder.

Habitat:

Elephants are found in various habitats including savannas, forests, and grasslands across Africa and Asia.

Behavior:

Elephants are social animals that live in herds led by a matriarch. They communicate through vocalizations and body language.

Diet:

Elephants are herbivores and primarily eat grasses, leaves, fruits, and bark. They can consume up to 150 kg (330 lbs) of food in a single day.

Reproduction:

Elephants have a long gestation period of about 22 months, and typically give birth to a single calf.

Conservation Status:

Elephants are listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and poaching for their ivory tusks.

Interesting Facts:

Elephants can recognize themselves in mirrors and are known for their excellent memory.
